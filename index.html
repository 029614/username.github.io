My Journey So Far!

  • Growing Up

    I was born in Federal Way, Washington, on October 3rd, 1992. When I was three, my family moved back to Alaska, to a town called Nikiski, where I lived until I left home at seventeen. I spent my childhood playing in true wilderness, and grew to become an excellent woodsman and survivalist (It's too bad such skills are irrelevent this century).

  • Mt Spurr Cabinets: A Family Business

    For as long as I can remember, I've worked in my Father's cabinet shop. Sweeping, organizing, gophering (Go for "this"! Go for "that"!), and eventually becoming a competant craftsman myself.

  • Fish and Ships

    It's an age old story in an age of age old stories, the one about the kid who thinks he's ready to make his own decisions. Not long after I turned 17, I said goodbye to the parents and left for what would be a stern lesson in reality.

    I found work aboard my cousins fishing boat and spent time commerical fishing in the Gulf of Alaska. This led me to yet another fishing boat which led me ultimately to the United States Merchant Marine.

  • Around the World in 4 Years

    In December of 2013, I left home for a seafaring apprenticeship, my ticket to adventure on the high seas. In the four years I spent at sea, I didn't see all that much adventure. Mostly I saw water to the horizon in every direction, and found that life at sea, in this day, is more peaceful then anything. It was all going well until a girl, my now wife, destroyed everything I thought I loved.

  • Bringing It Home

    They say the greatest killer of sailors by far is love, and at least in my case, it was true.

    I moved home in 2017 and began sailing aboard ships which worked locally only, in order to be closer to my wife. I had been considering a career in software for many years, even before I had ever left for sea, and a career change was needed. Thats when I tore my ACL and Meniscus.

    The break from work was the perfect excuse to start my new life in software and I quickly signed up for and eventually completed an online bootcamp with Thinkful.
